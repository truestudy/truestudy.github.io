1. 1. Why is the TV called Plasma TV? 2. How much electricity power does a plasma TV consume? 3. What happens if the screen of the TV is pierced or cracked? 4. Which one is brighter, Plasma TV or LCD YV? 5. What do you think of Plasma TV? ( Give at least 3 opinions)


1. Why is the TV called Plasma TV? It is called "plasma" displays because the technology utilizes small cells containing electrically discharged ionized gases, more commonly known as fluorescent lamps.

2. How much electricity power does a plasma TV consume?  Typical power consumption is 400 watts for a 127 cm (50 inch) screen and 200 to 310 watts for a 127 cm (50 inch) display when the TV is set to cinema mode.

3. What happens if the screen of the TV is pierced or cracked?  If the screen is pierced or cracked, the gas inside instantly cools down to prevent major injuries or fire.

4. Which one is brighter, Plasma TV or LCD TV?  Plasma TV is brighter than LCD TV.

5. What do you think of Plasma TV? ( Give at least 3 opinions)

In my opinion, Plasma TV displays are bright even brighter than LCD TV, have a wide color gamut, and can be produced in fairly large size up to 3.8 meters diagonally.
