Jawaban:

Δ Question Δ

(1) Jelly fish are not really fish. They are invertebrate animals. This mean that unlike fish or people, they have no backbones. In fact they no have bones at all.

(2) Jellyfish have stomach and mouths, but no heads. They have nervous systems for the sensing the world around them, but no brains. They are made almost entirely of water, which is why you can look though them.

(3) Some jellyfish can glow in darkness by making their own light. The light is made by a chemical reaction inside the jellyfish. Scientists belive jellyfish glow for several reasons. For example, they may glow to scare away predators or to attact animals they like to eat.

(4) Most jellyfish live in salt water, apart from a few types that live in fresh water. Jellyfish are found in oceans and seas all over the world. They live in warm, tropical seas and in icy waters near the North and South poles.

~Questions

1. Which one creates Jellyfish's light?

A. White blood

B. Nervous system

C. Chemical reaction

D. Salt water

D. Icy waters

2. Which one is true about the jellyfish based on the text?

A. They belong to invertebrate animals

B. They have heads like other animals

C. Their brain helps them find the food

D. They cannot live in fresh water

E. They eat seaweed

Δ Answer Δ

1. C. Chemical reaction

2. A. They belong to invertebrate animals

Δ Explanation Δ

1) Ikan ubur-ubur bukanlah benar-benar ikan. Mereka adalah hewan invertebrata. Ini berarti bahwa tidak seperti ikan atau manusia, mereka tidak memiliki tulang punggung. Bahkan mereka tidak memiliki tulang sama sekali.

(2) Ubur-ubur memiliki perut dan mulut, tetapi tidak memiliki kepala. Mereka memiliki sistem saraf untuk merasakan dunia di sekitar mereka, tetapi tidak memiliki otak. Mereka hampir seluruhnya terbuat dari air, itulah sebabnya Anda dapat melihatnya.

(3) Beberapa ubur-ubur dapat bersinar dalam kegelapan dengan membuat cahayanya sendiri. Cahaya dibuat oleh reaksi kimia di dalam ubur-ubur. Para ilmuwan percaya ubur-ubur bersinar karena beberapa alasan. Misalnya, mereka mungkin bersinar untuk menakut-nakuti pemangsa atau untuk menyerang hewan yang mereka sukai.

(4) Sebagian besar ubur-ubur hidup di air asin, selain beberapa jenis yang hidup di air tawar. Ubur-ubur ditemukan di lautan dan lautan di seluruh dunia. Mereka hidup di laut tropis yang hangat dan di perairan es di dekat kutub Utara dan Selatan.
