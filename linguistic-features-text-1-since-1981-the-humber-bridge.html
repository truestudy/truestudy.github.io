10. Pick an example of a descriptive text and analyze the linguistic features it has!​


Jawaban:

Here is an example of a descriptive text about a city and an analysis of its linguistic features:

Example:

New York City is a bustling metropolis located in the northeastern United States. The city is known for its iconic skyline, which includes landmarks such as the Empire State Building and the One World Trade Center. It is also home to world-class museums, theaters, and restaurants, as well as famous parks like Central Park and the High Line. The city has a diverse population and a rich cultural history, with neighborhoods like Chinatown, Little Italy, and Harlem each offering their own unique flavor. Overall, New York City is a vibrant and exciting destination that offers something for everyone.

Analysis:

The above descriptive text is about New York City and uses language to convey the unique features and characteristics of the city. Here are some linguistic features that are notable in this text:

1. Adjectives: The text makes use of a variety of descriptive adjectives to give the reader a sense of the city's atmosphere, such as "bustling," "iconic," "world-class," "famous," "diverse," "rich," "unique," and "vibrant." These adjectives help to create an image of New York City in the reader's mind.

2. Nouns: The text also uses a variety of specific and proper nouns to name key features of the city, such as "Empire State Building," "One World Trade Center," "Central Park," "High Line," "Chinatown," "Little Italy," and "Harlem." By using these nouns, the writer is able to provide more detail about the city and make it feel more tangible to the reader.

3. Metaphors: Although the text doesn't use many explicit metaphors, it does make comparisons between different parts of the city to give the reader a sense of their character. For example, it describes the neighborhoods of Chinatown, Little Italy, and Harlem as each having their own "unique flavor," which is a metaphorical way of describing their distinct cultures and histories.

4. Active voice: The text uses the active voice to create a sense of immediacy and action. For example, it describes New York City as "a bustling metropolis," which gives the impression that the city is always moving and evolving.

5. Pronouns: The text makes use of pronouns like "it" and "its" to refer back to the city and create cohesion throughout the text. This helps to ensure that the reader remains focused on the subject of the text and doesn't get distracted by other details.

12. Generic Structure, Linguistic Features Biography Text Apa saja? . tolong sama sumber bacaannya.


Biography text is a kind of text which describes a person's life and written by someone else in detail.

The purpose of biography text :

To understand a person’s story of life outside of any accomplishments this person may be known for. To enable many information and educate the readers.

Pembahasan

Generic Structure of biography text :

Orientation

=> It is the opening paragraph. It tells the background information of the person. Usually, it consists of a person's identity such as full name, address and date of birth.

Events

=> It is written in chronological order. This paragraph tells about the event happened to the person.  

Re - orientation (optional)

=> It consists of conclusion or a comment or the writer. It also tells about the achievement or the contribution of the person.

Linguistic features of biography text :

Use simple past tense

Example : He was born on August 17, in Jakarta, Indonesia.

Use time sequence

Example : His parents were divorced when he was three years old.

Focus on specific participant

Example : Abraham Lincoln was born in 1809 in Kentucky (USA). He worked on the farm of his father.

Use action verb

Example : He did different types of jobs before he settled as a highly successful lawyer.

Example of the biography text :

Abraham Lincoln

Abraham Lincoln was born in 1809 in Kentucky (USA). He worked on the farm of his father. He attended school for less than a year, but taught himself to read and write. He did different types of jobs before he settled as a highly successful lawyer. He was gradually drawn to politics.

The country was having problems regarding the practice of slavery. Therefore, there was no place for slavery in that country,

At this difficult time, Abraham Lincoln was elected President of the USA in 1860. He wanted to solve the problem of slavery. The southern states were against the abolition of slavery. This brought the unity of the country in danger. The southern states were prepared even to form a new country. Abraham Lincoln wanted all the states to remain united.

He faced many problems. He wanted to preserve the unity of the country at any cost. Finally a civil war broke out between the northern and southern states. He fought the war bravely and declared, 'A Nation cannot exist half free and half slave.' He won the war and kept the country united.

Lincoln was elected president for a second term. He was not against anybody and wanted everybody to live in peace. He made sincere efforts to heal the people's wounds caused by the war. In 1862, Lincoln declared that from then onwards all slaves would be free. This made him very popular among the people. Lincoln was assassinated in 1865.

1. Descriptive text means one type of English text which contains a detailed description or picture of an object. Usually the object described can be inanimate objects, places,

2. Identification: Contains a general statement of the object to be described by the author.

Description Section: Contains a detailed description or description of the object being described.

Closing: Contains conclusions from the author's impression of the object.

3. Use nouns according to the topic being described. Such as: hospital, house, my car, and others.

Use phrases that contain nouns. For example: he is a kind-hearted head of the lab, and others.

Contains adjectives that describe something. Such as: two diligent students, three white socks, and so on.

Contains transitive verbs to provide subject information. For example: The girl is wearing a brown uniform.

Contains a verb (feeling, opinion) with the aim of expressing the author's personal view on a subject. Like: I think it's a smart chicken, I believe the clothes are cheap, and so on.

Contains adverbs to provide additional information about an object. Like: sooner or later, at the coffee shop, at the hospital, and so on.

Contains figurative language in the form of a parable or metaphor. Such as: His skin is pure white like white cotton.
