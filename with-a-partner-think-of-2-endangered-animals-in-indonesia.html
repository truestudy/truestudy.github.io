Adult males have a single horn and females have none, unlike the two-horned African and Sumatran rhinos. Once common in Southeast Asia, they now exist only in Ujung Kulon National Park on the southwestern tip of Java. This tiny habitat leaves them susceptible to disease, in-breeding and the threat of natural disasters. They have long been hunted as big-game trophies and poached for their small horns, which are prized in China as an aphrodisiac by stupid men with flaccid penises. 2.Sumatran Rhino : The world’s smallest rhino species. Known for their two horns and hairy bodies, they once occupied vast areas of East Asia but are now confined to a few pockets of Sumatra and Kalimantan. Deforestation and poaching are driving them to extinction.

25. 4. Putting animals in captivity is the best way to preserve endangered animals. Do you agree with the statement? Why/ Why not?​


Captivity can be beneficial for the preservation of some endangered animal species, but it is not the only solution, nor is it always the best option. One benefit of captivity is that it allows for controlled breeding programs, which can help increase the population size of a species. It also allows for the close monitoring and medical care of animals, which can help improve their overall health and survival rates. Additionally, captive breeding programs can act as a safety net against extinction in case wild populations are unable to recover.

However, keeping animals in captivity can also have negative effects on their well-being, such as loss of natural behaviors, reduced genetic diversity, and increased susceptibility to diseases. Furthermore, releasing captive-bred animals into the wild can be challenging, and they may not be able to survive on their own.

Overall, captive breeding and care can be an important tool for conservation, but it should be part of a broader conservation strategy that also includes protecting and restoring wild habitats, reducing human impacts on wild populations, and addressing the underlying causes of species decline.
