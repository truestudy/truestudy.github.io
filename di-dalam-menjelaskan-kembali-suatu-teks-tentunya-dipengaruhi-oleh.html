Tsunami

1. General Statement

Tsunami is a term derived from Japanese, consisting of two words "tsu" and "nami", each of which means "harbor" and "wave". Meanwhile, scientists interpret it as a "tide" (tidal wave) or seismic seismic waves (seismic sea waves).

Tsunami is a big ocean wave that comes quickly and suddenly hit the coastal area. Waves are formed as a result of aktvitas earthquake or Mount Merapi erupt under the sea. The magnitude of the tsunami waves caused flooding and damage when it hit the coast.

2. Explanation areas (contents):

The formation of a tsunami occurs when the seabed rises and falls along the fault during the earthquake. The fault resulted in disruption of sea water balance. Large fractures will generate a large wave power as well. Moments after the earthquake, the water subsided. After receding, sea water returns to the mainland in the form of big waves. In addition, the formation of tsunamis is also caused by the eruption of Mount Merapi on the ocean floor. The eruption caused a high movement of sea water or surrounding waters. The bigger the tsunami, the greater the flood or damage that occurs when hit the beach.

The speed of tsunami waves is greater than normal waves in general, which can go up to 700 km / hour, almost equivalent to the rate of aircraft. The speed will decrease when the tsunami waves enter the shallow ocean, but the wave height will increase. Tsunami wave height is generally 50 to 100 meters and spread in all directions. In addition, the height of the tsunami waves is also affected by the shape of the beach and its depth. Earthquakes on the ocean floor have the potential to create a tsunami that is harmful to humans.

3. Conclusion / Conclusion (enterpretation):

Tsunami has indeed become one of the disasters that cause great damage to humans. The greatest damage occurred when the tsunami hit the settlements so that it dragged whatever it went through. Therefore, we must always be vigilant and prepare for this disaster. However, we need not worry too much because not all tsunamis form big waves. In addition, not all volcanic eruptions or earthquakes that followed followed by the tsunami.
