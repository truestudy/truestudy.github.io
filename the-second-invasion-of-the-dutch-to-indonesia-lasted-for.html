27. West Irian Joined Republic of Indonesia The liberation of West Irian was one of the biggest issues of sovereignty at the beginning of the independence period of the Republic of Indonesia. This conflict arose when the Dutch colonialist was unwilling to let West Irian go into the NKRI section. The Indonesian government struggled through various methods ranging from diplomacy to the military. On December 19, 1961, the government issued “Tri Komando Rakyat (Trikora)” statements which determined to prevent the formation of a Papuan puppet state mad by the Dutch, to hoist the Indonesia flag in the entire West Irian area, and to prepare for general mobilization to maintain the independence and unity of the homeland and the nation. Ever since then, the confrontation with the Dutch began. On January 2, 1962, President Soekarno issued Decree No. 1 of 1962 to form the Mandala Command for Liberation of West Irian. In 1969, a referendum was held for the West Irian people. The result stated that the people in West Irian wanted to join the Republic of Indonesia. The success of “Trikora” was the result of military and diplomacy cooperation. On May 4, 1963, three days after the return of West Irian to Indonesia, President Soekarno addressed a crowd of thousands of West Irian people in Kota Baru, Jayapura stating that West Irian joined the Republic of Indonesia.
